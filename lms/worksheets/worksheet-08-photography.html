The 4 Technical Skills
1
Camera Settings (Exposure)
Understand three things: Shutter Speed (how fast), Aperture (how wide the lens opens), and ISO (how sensitive to light). These three work together for every photo.
2
Composition & Framing
Use the Rule of Thirds — mentally divide the frame into nine squares and place your subject at the intersection points. Avoid placing subjects dead centre.
3
Lighting
Natural window light is free and powerful. For portraits, position the subject facing the light source. Harsh midday sun creates unflattering shadows — use shade or golden hour light.
4
Editing & Delivery
Adjust brightness, contrast, and colour balance using editing apps. Deliver clean, well-named files to the client promptly. Your editing style becomes your brand.
